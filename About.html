Waterfalls are powerful natural phenomena where river water drops abruptly over a steep cliff,
formed by erosion of softer rock beneath harder rock,
creating a misty, roaring spectacle that supports unique ecosystems with specialized plants and animals,
offers beautiful microclimates, and serves as geological markers of erosion,
all while providing a powerful symbol of nature's constant flow, beauty, and enduring force
